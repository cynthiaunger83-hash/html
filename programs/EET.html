what is EET?

is a hands-on course that teaches you how to work with eletrical systems, eletronics, and technology used in the real world.

The Electrical Engineering Technology program at Erie County Technical School prepares students for real-world careers by teaching how electrical systems work and how to install, maintain, and repair them. Students learn about wiring, circuits, and how electricity is used in homes, businesses, and industries. In this program, students get hands-on experience working with tools, equipment, and electrical components. They also learn safety practices, problem-solving, and how to read diagrams and blueprints, which are important skills for electricians and technicians. Overall, the program helps students build practical skills they can use after graduation. They can go straight into a job, start an apprenticeship, or continue their education in electrical or engineering fields.
