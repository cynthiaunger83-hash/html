what is CMN?

it provides a foundation in information technology(it) by teatching students how to build, secure, and maintain complex digital systems, it is designed for students who enjoy logical problem-solving and want to understand how computers communicate.

The Computer Networking program at Erie County Technical School prepares students for real-world tech careers by teaching how computers connect and communicate. Students learn how to set up networks, manage internet connections, and keep systems running smoothly, which are important skills in today’s technology-driven world. In this program, students get hands-on experience working with computers, cables, and networking equipment. They also learn problem-solving, cybersecurity basics, and how to fix network issues, which helps them understand what it’s like to work in IT. Overall, the program gives students practical skills they can use after graduation. They can go straight into an entry-level IT job, continue their education, or work toward industry certifications in networking and technology.
