The Emergency and Protective Services program at Erie County Technical School prepares students for real-world careers in public safety. Students learn about police work, firefighting, emergency response, and how to handle different types of emergencies. In this program, students get hands-on experience with training activities like basic first aid, teamwork drills, and emergency simulations. They also learn communication, leadership, and how to stay calm under pressure, which are very important in emergency situations. Overall, the program helps students build practical skills they can use after graduation. They can go into careers like law enforcement, firefighting, emergency medical services, or continue their education in public safety fields.
