it focuses on the "mechanical brain" and "heart" of the vehical. while AUR program handels th ebody and paint, AUT is all about diagnosing, maitaining, and reparing internal systems.

The Automotive Technology program at Erie County Technical School teaches students how to maintain and repair vehicles so they’re ready for real-world jobs. Students learn how engines, brakes, and electrical systems work, and how to diagnose and fix problems in cars and trucks. In this program, students get hands-on experience working with tools and real vehicles in a shop setting. They also learn safety, problem-solving, and how to use diagnostic equipment, which are all important skills for working as a mechanic. Overall, the program helps students build practical skills they can use after graduation. They can go straight into a job, continue training, or work toward certifications in the automotive field.
